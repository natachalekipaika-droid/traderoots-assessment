Good to know
This is not an exam — there is no pass or fail. It is a diagnostic you can return to at any point in your trading journey, whenever you want to check where you stand and what to work on next. Many SMEs in the programme retake it every few months and watch the score move as the business grows. Come back to it as often as you like.
What “export ready” actually means: a business with the character, capacity and courage to compete beyond its home market — not just the ambition to. It describes a firm that can take the information, resources and support on offer and turn them into results, and that knows how to work through the unknowns of a new market rather than being stopped by them (adapted from Food Export USA Northeast, 2002). Readiness is also market-specific: a business can be genuinely ready to trade into Kenya and still have work to do before it's ready for Egypt.
For a non-exporter, this Export Readiness Index (ERI) is a chance to understand what preparedness looks like; for exporters, it sharpens awareness of real strengths and gaps.

Answer honestly — this is your baseline, not a test. Wherever a question asks about something you already have in place, you'll be asked to back it up with a short explanation or a document, so your score reflects where your business really is today.
